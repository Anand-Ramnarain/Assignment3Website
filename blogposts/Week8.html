Readings:

Towards a study of information geographies: (im)mutable augmentations and a mapping of the geographies of information


Information geographies refer to the spatial dimensions of information flows and their impact on societies. cultures. and economies. (Graham & Sabbata & Zook 2015).meaning that information geographies are concerned with the way information moves and is distributed across different spaces or parts of the world and how this movement affects societies.

The concept of "(im)mutable augmentations" (Graham & Sabbata & Zook 2015). suggests that the digital realm can store and disseminate vast amounts of information, which can have empowering and constraining effects. On one hand, it can be argued that individual capabilities by providing access to knowledge and resources. But it can also amplify existing inequalities and power imbalances.

In the article, they talk about the “mapping geographies of access” (Graham & Sabbata & Zook 2015: 102).which is about about the uneven geographies of geographic information as this highlights that geographic information is not evenly distributed or accessible. Showing that there are disparities in the availability, production, and usage of geographic information across different regions and user groups.

In summary, it emphasizes the uneven distribution of geographic information and the importance of studying both the front-end platforms and back-end databases, as it calls for a critical examination of power dynamics and visibility within information geographies to strive for a more inclusive and equitable landscape.


Digital Hegemonies: The Localness of Search Engine Results


The concept of "digital hegemonies" (Ballatore & Graham &Sen 2017) implies that there is control applied on certain platforms in the digital realm. In the context of digital media, it refers to the power and influence wielded by search engines, such as Google, in shaping the information that users encounter when conducting searches.

The phrase "the localness of search engine results" (Ballatore & Graham &Sen 2017). and the ways explained in the article indicate that search engine algorithms consider various factors related to a user's geographical location in the search results. As search engines aim to provide personalized and relevant information to users, and they achieve this by using the user's location and giving them results to this. This means of using the user’s location results in information access that can change perspectives depending on what they are exposed to.


How this relates to and impact web development in SA?


Awareness of Information Disparities and Internet Connectivity: In the reading on information geographies highlights that there is an even distribution of information globally. In South Africa, there are significant disparities in access to technology and internet connectivity, as I experience many times that internet connectivity does not work and I must wait till it comes back online, this affects everyone but in terms of web developers, this creates a digital divide as they must work harder towards creating inclusive web experiences that the users can access in different regions and with different internet capabilities.

It is part of our duty as web developers in the Global South to boost representation and engagement in the digital material we are involved in producing. This can entail enticing South Africans to join in online debates and creating websites that are as platform-compatible as feasible. Bridging the information gap between the global North and South will assist ensure a more equitable distribution of knowledge.
