Readings:

Towards Decolonising Computational Sciences


When we talk about the decolonizing of computational sciences this is referred to as taking necessary steps towards creating a more inclusive and acceptable field that highlights the diversifying of perspectives, systems, and methodologies. (Birhane & Guest 2020) In the article there are two focuses the first is the “realization of the present-day system's behaviour towards women of colour” (Birhane & Guest 2020: 1) This refers to the understanding that the current system, has inherited and continues to be a hostile and oppressive environment, that foster behaviours and principles specifically targeted at women of colour. Indicating that women face challenges and discrimination due to the intersection of their gender and race. The other focus is on “the rejection of centring people as a solution” (Birhane & Guest 2020: 1) This implies that focusing solely on one person's experiences is not enough to address the problems faced by women of colour. Instead, it suggests that there are larger issues that need greater solutions. It needs to address the discrimination and inequality against people and women of colour. In the article the concept of “decolonization in the context of computational sciences” (Birhane & Guest 2020) and needing to address the obstacles that prevent Black women and other marginalized groups from participating in fields. It emphasizes that decolonizing the system requires individuals to decolonize themselves. highlighting the importance of investigating and dismantling the historical and current racist and oppressive roots formed in computational sciences. As this process will be gradual but necessary for the progression of the field. Showing this will not only will it benefit Black women, but it will also contribute to the overall advancement of computer science.
