Making A Feminist Internet In Africa: Why The Internet Needs African Feminists And Feminisms


The article "Making A Feminist Internet In Africa: Why The Internet Needs African Feminists And Feminisms" by Sheena Magenya, focuses on the Gendered Digital Divide, the culture in Africa, and how a feminist will approach the Internet and how it promotes social justice and gender equality. The close reading is concentrated on how technology and the internet function in our society and how gender interacts with it. It shows how there is gender-based violence and discrimination and how it is prevalent on African websites today and that a feminist strategy is required to solve these problems and guarantee that the internet is accessible and usable to all.

The Imagine a Feminist Internet Meeting was followed by a few developments. There weren't enough female voices online, despite the increase. This is seen in the gender-based violence on the internet. (Magenya, 2020) African feminists working towards combating this violence by using strong legal frameworks, digital literacy programs, and community support systems. It aims to create safer online spaces that allow freedom of expression and protect women's rights to participate without fear of intimidation or violence.

African Feminists will not only help inform others, but by sharing their life stories they can form communities which would help people with similar experiences. This will create a more meaningful experience for users online and reverse cycles of oppression (Magenya, 2020). As it is building solidarities. Through stories and feminist movements, there are various perspectives, experiences, and struggles. The internet is the tool used in connecting feminist activists across the continent. This allows African feminists to leverage digital networks to build communities which can exchange knowledge, and talk to form a collective to act, ultimately strengthening the feminist movement and its impact.
