What are APIs


APIs known as Application Programming Interfaces, are sets of rules and protocols that allow different software applications to communicate with each other. They are used to define the methods and data that developers can use to interact with a particular software component.




Readings:

Traveling While Trans


There are numerous problems faced by marginalized groups as emphasized in Costanza Chock's reading on travelling while trans. The reading emphasizes these problems face and the issues concerning the design justice system as well as ways to address these issues. Gaps in design can be filled through cooperation, according to the Design Justice Network (Costanza-Chock 2020: 6).

A one-time conversation won't be able to address all the problems that marginalized people confront, thus this partnership should continue. To change systems, this cooperation is essential (Costanza-Chock 2020: 6). As in terms of the reading and the tech industry, this cooperation can play a role in developing solutions and platforms that provide resources and support for transgender travellers or people in the LGBT community. This can involve creating travel-related applications, websites, or online communities that offer information on transgender-friendly destinations, local LGBTQ+ resources, and safety tips for trans individuals while travelling.

Designers don't always intend to disadvantage or discriminate against people when they build items. However, this is still what their designs do. (Costanza-Chock 2020: 5) This can lead to exclusive environments, but rather with cooperation and trying to be more open to creating inclusive and diverse environments where everyone, regardless of their gender identity or background, feels welcome and supported.
